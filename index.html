The Craft

How I Build

I move fast, prototype faster, and obsess over how a game feels in the player's hands. Here's the toolkit behind the work.

01

Start with the Spark

Every project begins with a question: what should this feel like to play? I sketch the core loop on paper before opening the engine — if the idea doesn't excite me in one sentence, it won't survive a year of development.

02

Prototype Fast, Fail Faster

I get a playable build in days, not weeks. Ugly cubes, placeholder sounds, no polish — just the mechanic in your hands. If the prototype isn't fun with grey boxes, no amount of art will save it.

03

Design the Loop

Once the core works, I shape the experience around it — pacing, progression, difficulty curves, the moments of tension and relief. The goal is the loop players don't notice but can't stop playing.

04

Build It Right

Clean C# in Unity, Blueprints in Unreal — whichever fits the project. I write code that the next developer (often future me) can read without cursing. Component-based architecture, no spaghetti, no surprises.

05

Test, Listen, Iterate

I put builds in real players' hands as early as possible. Their first reaction tells me more than a month of polish. What I think is fun and what actually is fun are rarely the same — playtesting closes that gap.

06

Ship It Smooth

The last 10% is what separates a game from a product. Performance profiling, bug hunting, polish, store assets, launch trailers — the unglamorous work that decides whether anyone actually plays what you built.
